# Voice & Tone

Our voice is the personality behind every word. It's how we sound whether we're writing a billboard or an email subject line.

---

## Brand Personality

We are **confident** but never arrogant. **Expert** but never condescending. **Warm** but never unprofessional. We speak like the advisor you'd actually want to have coffee with — someone who's been there, done that, and genuinely wants to help.

## Core Voice Attributes

- **Direct** — Lead with the point. No corporate filler.
- **Human** — Write like a person, not an institution.
- **Knowing** — We have the data and the experience to back it up.
- **Optimistic** — We believe in the businesses we serve.

---

## Messaging Framework

### Tagline
**Your Business, Better.**

### Campaign Pillars

- **Your Business, Connected** — Network, resources, community
- **Your Business, People** — Advisors, mentors, real humans
- **Your Business, Funded** — Capital access, loans, grants

### Headline Patterns

Headlines use bold serif type with *italic emphasis* on the emotional hook:

- "What's free advice worth? $547 million, *last year.*"
- "Your business deserves someone who *gets it.*"
- "Don't settle for *generic advice.*"
- "Real clients. Real *results.*"

---

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Homepage hero | Bold, aspirational | "Your business deserves someone who gets it." |
| Impact stats | Confident, data-driven | "Since 1980, we've helped raise $2.8B in capital." |
| Client stories | Warm, authentic | "When Maria came to us, she had a recipe and a dream." |
| Social media | Conversational, punchy | "42,000 jobs created. And counting." |
| Email outreach | Professional, helpful | "We noticed you haven't scheduled your free consultation." |
